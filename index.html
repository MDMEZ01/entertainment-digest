Netflix Is Reinventing Cable — And the Irony Is That This Is the Only Move Left

Why It Matters
  • Netflix is simultaneously exploring linear TV channels inside the app AND becoming a platform hub where users subscribe to other streaming services through Netflix — both moves are a direct replication of what cable TV did for 40 years
    • The company that disrupted cable by eliminating its bundle, its linear grid, and its ad load is now actively rebuilding all three — but with a 283M-household subscriber base as its distribution advantage
    • Dylan Byers (Puck): Sarandos and Peters are “throwing all manner of ideas against the wall” as engagement declines and YouTube eats an increasing share of TV time (13%+ of U.S. smart TV vs. Netflix’s 7.8%)
  • Sean McNulty (The Wakeup) makes the most compelling operational case: Netflix channels would address the “lean-back factor” that cable and YouTube currently own — the 11 PM “I just want to put something on for 30 minutes” viewer whom Netflix has never captured
    • Netflix already holds one of the world’s deepest libraries of “gathering dust on a server shelf” original programming that channels could revive without new production spend
The Money
  • Netflix’s ad revenue was $1.5B in 2025; management targets $3B in 2026 — linear channels would open entirely new AVOD inventory without requiring original content investment
    • The platform-hub strategy (letting users subscribe to other apps through Netflix) generates revenue share with no content cost; Amazon Prime Video Channels reportedly generates $3B+ annually
    • If Peacock becomes a Netflix sub-app, the implications for standalone NBCU’s Q3 subscriber targets — and its M&A value — are immediate and significant
Historical Context
  • The closest precedent is the cable MSO model of the 1980s–1990s: a single infrastructure player that aggregated all video content, took a cut of subscriber fees, and used scale to dictate terms to content owners
    • Netflix’s subscriber scale (283M households) and first-party viewing data would make it the most powerful cable operator in history — without the physical infrastructure costs
Watch For
  • Netflix Q2 earnings call next week — any language from Sarandos or Peters about “platform” strategy or “distribution infrastructure” will be the clearest signal that channels and hub plans are advancing toward announcement
    • Whether Peacock is named as a potential sub-app candidate, and how NBCU’s standalone management responds publicly, will tell you everything about the state of the streaming ecosystem’s power dynamics heading into 2027
  • September 15: Cloudflare changes AI crawler defaults, potentially reshaping how streaming platforms’ content metadata and recommendation engines index and surface programming — a sleeper issue for Netflix’s discovery architecture
