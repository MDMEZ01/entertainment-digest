Why It Matters
  • Matthew Belloni’s definitive Moana autopsy (Puck, July 13) reveals that the live-action film’s failure was not a creative miscalculation — it was the downstream consequence of a deliberate financial decision Bob Iger made in February 2024 to protect his short-term position as CEO
  • Facing activist investor Nelson Peltz and wanting a quick win to replace Elio (which Iger privately knew was a failure), Iger personally announced Moana 2 for November 2024 — just 19 months before the planned live-action film; that decision cannibalized the audience for the remake before it existed
  • The structural implication is chilling: the person running Disney made a decision that maximized his near-term position (investor confidence, $1B quick box office for Moana 2) while creating the conditions for a $100M+ loss he would not be accountable for — “It is now Josh D’Amaro’s problem, not his,” writes Belloni
