📈 Today’s Deep Dive

Why the WGA’s Long-Shot Injunction Is Really a Fight Over the Shrinking Market for Writers

    Why It Matters
  • By filing its own injunction, the WGA reframes the Paramount–WBD deal from a theatrical-distribution question into a labor one — who gets to bid for scripts.
  • The guild’s claim of a 35% combined share of top-film writing services stacks a second antitrust theory on top of the states’ distribution case, widening the legal front against the merger.
  • If accepted, the argument could become a template for unions to challenge future media consolidation on monopsony (single-buyer) grounds.
    • The Money
  • Every day past Sept. 30 that the ~$111B deal stays unclosed triggers a $7M-per-day “ticking fee” largely bankrolled by Larry Ellison.
  • WBD trades near $25.80 — a 17% discount to Paramount’s $31 all-cash offer — showing the market now prices in real closing risk.
    • Historical Context
  • Fewer buyers for creative labor echoes the post-consolidation squeeze writers fought in the 2023 strike; the WGA is now testing that grievance in an antitrust court.
    • Watch For
  • The Aug. 3 hearing, where the judge weighs the states’ market-share theory and whether the guild’s case rides alongside it.
  • A pending EU decision and whether Paramount seeks appellate review before the ticking fee bites.
